Electrical Motors Explained

AC and DC Motor Working Principles

What is an Electrical Motor?

An electrical motor converts electrical energy into mechanical motion using magnetic fields. It works based on a fundamental physics principle called the Lorentz Force:

Force = Magnetic Field × Current × Length

When electric current flows through a wire in a magnetic field, a force is produced that causes motion.

DC Motor Working Principle

A DC motor runs on Direct Current (DC) electricity.

Main Components:

  • Stator (Permanent magnets)
  • Rotor (Armature coil)
  • Commutator
  • Brushes
  • DC Power Supply

How it Works:

  1. DC current flows into the armature coil
  2. The coil creates a magnetic field
  3. This interacts with stator magnetic field
  4. Force is generated
  5. Rotor starts spinning
  6. Commutator reverses current to maintain rotation

AC Motor Working Principle

AC motors run on Alternating Current (AC). Instead of using a commutator, they use a rotating magnetic field.

Main Components:

  • Stator (AC powered coils)
  • Rotor
  • AC Power Supply

How it Works:

  1. AC current flows in stator coils
  2. Creates rotating magnetic field
  3. This induces current in rotor
  4. Rotor produces its own magnetic field
  5. Rotor follows rotating field
  6. Motor spins continuously
