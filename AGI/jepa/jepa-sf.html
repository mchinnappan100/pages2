Foundation

What JEPA Does Differently

01

"JEPA learns by predicting abstract representations of the world — not just pixel-level or token-level patterns. This makes it far more efficient and capable of reasoning with less labeled data."

— Yann LeCun, Meta AI / NYU
Conventional Generative AI
  • Predicts in raw token / pixel space
  • Averages over all plausible outputs → blurry or hallucinated responses
  • Requires massive labeled datasets per task
  • Pattern-matching: no internal world model
  • Brittle under distribution shift
  • No long-horizon planning — one-step generation
  • High hallucination risk in factual enterprise settings
JEPA World-Model AI
  • Predicts in abstract representation space
  • Encodes only predictable, semantically meaningful structure
  • Self-supervised: learns from unlabeled data at scale
  • Builds an internal world model — reasons about state and context
  • Transfers robustly across tasks and domains
  • Enables multi-step, hierarchical planning (H-JEPA)
  • Grounded predictions reduce hallucination
🔮

Representation Space Prediction

Instead of predicting the next token word-for-word, JEPA predicts the abstract representation of what comes next — filtering out irrelevant noise and focusing on semantic content.

Encoder abstracts: customer intent, sentiment, deal stage — not raw words
Predictor forecasts: next interaction type, churn risk, close probability
Result: crisp, semantically grounded predictions — not averaged-out guesses
🌍

Internal World Model

JEPA builds an internal model of how the world (or in this case, the customer relationship and business process) evolves — enabling reasoning about future states without executing them.

Models customer journey states and transitions
Simulates "what if" scenarios before recommending actions
Gradient-based planning: no trial-and-error in production
💡

Self-Supervised on CRM Data

The vast majority of enterprise CRM data is unlabeled — interaction logs, emails, case notes, opportunity histories. JEPA can learn from all of it without annotation.

Pretrain on raw Salesforce interaction history — zero labels needed
Fine-tune with a small labeled set for the specific task
10⁵× more training signal per record vs supervised labeling
Salesforce CRM unlabeled data available for SSL: ✅ Email threads (tone, urgency, intent) ✅ Case notes (problem type, resolution path) ✅ Opportunity history (stage transitions, velocity) ✅ Activity logs (call, meeting, demo patterns) ✅ Chatter messages (internal context signals) JEPA pretext task: Mask: last 3 touchpoints in account timeline Predict: representation of those touchpoints → Model learns: customer journey structure → Fine-tune with 500 labeled churn examples → Outperforms model trained on 50,000 labeled rows

How JEPA Can Help Salesforce

02
🧠

1 · Smarter CRM Intelligence

JEPA's world-model approach better understands context across customer interactions — predicting customer intent, churn signals, and next-best actions with far greater accuracy than pattern-matching models.

Intent prediction: understand why a customer is contacting, not just what they said
Churn signals: reason over the full account timeline, not isolated events
Next-best action: simulate outcomes before recommending
Upsell timing: model customer readiness as a world state, not a score
Current approach (pattern matching): Feature: "support ticket count > 3" Output: churn_score = 0.72 Action: "assign to retention team" JEPA world-model approach: State: [account journey representation] Predict: representation of future state Simulate: "if we offer a discount → state X" "if we upgrade support tier → state Y" Choose: action that minimises churn energy → Context-aware, causal, multi-step reasoning

The JEPA model reasons about the trajectory of the customer relationship — not a single snapshot score.

⚡

2 · Enhanced Einstein AI

JEPA's architecture can power more robust reasoning in Einstein — moving beyond pattern matching to genuine understanding of business processes and customer journeys.

Einstein Copilot: answers grounded in world-model reasoning, not token prediction
Einstein Prediction Builder: JEPA features replace hand-crafted signal engineering
Semantic search across CRM records with JEPA embeddings
Cross-cloud reasoning: understand how Sales signals affect Service outcomes

A sales rep asks Einstein Copilot: "Why is Acme Corp likely to churn?" A JEPA-powered Einstein doesn't just retrieve documents — it reasons over the account's world-model state: declining engagement trajectory, unresolved escalation, contract anniversary approaching, and a competitor signal in recent email sentiment.

JEPA makes Einstein a reasoner, not a retriever.

📉

3 · Better Data Efficiency

Salesforce customers often have limited labeled data for their specific domain. JEPA learns effectively from unlabeled or sparse data — a huge win for enterprise AI models trained on proprietary CRM data.

SMB customers: viable AI with hundreds of labeled records, not millions
Niche industries (healthcare, legal, gov): scarce domain-labeled data is no longer a blocker
Rapid onboarding: new org gets strong AI from day one using SSL on raw history
Continual learning: model improves as unlabeled interactions accumulate
🤖

4 · Agentforce Evolution

JEPA-based models could make Agentforce agents more capable of long-horizon planning — handling multi-step workflows with better contextual awareness and far fewer errors.

H-JEPA hierarchy: agent plans at task level (close deal) and step level (send quote)
World-model simulation: agent simulates workflow paths before executing
Gradient-based planning replaces trial-and-error in agentic loops
Context persistence: maintains coherent state across multi-turn, multi-day workflows
Agentforce task: "Renew the Acme Corp contract" Current LLM-based agent: Step 1: search contract → ok Step 2: draft renewal email → ok Step 3: send email before approval → ❌ error JEPA H-JEPA agent: Level 2 (task): renewal → approval → send → sign Level 1 (steps): draft → legal review → exec sign-off World model: simulate each step before executing Cost module: penalise out-of-order actions → Completes workflow correctly, fewer errors ✅
🌐

5 · Multimodal Capabilities

JEPA naturally extends to vision + text + structured data, enabling richer AI across Slack, Documents, Tableau, and MuleSoft integrations — without redesigning the model per modality.

Slack: understand message tone + thread context + account state together
Salesforce Files / DocGen: reason over contract images + structured deal data
Tableau: joint reasoning over charts + underlying CRM data
MuleSoft: unified representation across heterogeneous API data sources
One JEPA backbone — multiple modality encoders feeding shared representation
🛡️

6 · Reduced Hallucinations

JEPA's predictive, structured world-model approach is inherently more grounded than generative-only models — improving trust and reliability in customer-facing and compliance-sensitive AI.

Predictions anchored to learned world-model state — not free-form generation
Energy function assigns low energy to implausible outputs — natural filter
No blurry averaging: abstract representation discards unpredictable noise
Critical for: financial services, healthcare, legal, public sector Salesforce use cases

An Einstein Copilot in a financial services org is asked to summarise a client's portfolio risk. A generative model might hallucinate a plausible-sounding but incorrect figure. A JEPA model's energy function assigns high energy (low confidence) to outputs inconsistent with the account's learned world-model state — and either abstains or flags uncertainty.

World-model grounding is especially valuable in regulated industries where Salesforce is heavily deployed.

JEPA vs Current Enterprise AI

05

✓ No annotation bottleneck

JEPA pretrain on raw CRM data means every Salesforce org — from 50-person SMB to Fortune 500 — can build powerful AI without expensive labeling programs.

Current: need 50,000 labeled churn examples JEPA SSL: pretrain on all interaction history Fine-tune: 500 labeled examples → same accuracy ✅

✓ Causal, multi-step reasoning

World-model simulation lets the AI reason about what will happen next across multiple steps — not just pattern-match on the current state.

LLM agent: execute step → observe → repeat JEPA agent: simulate all steps first choose lowest-cost path execute confidently ✅

✓ Transfer across Salesforce clouds

One pretrained JEPA model can be fine-tuned for Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, and Industry Clouds — sharing representations across all products.

One JEPA backbone → multiple fine-tuned heads: Sales Cloud: opportunity scoring Service Cloud: case deflection Health Cloud: patient risk stratification Financial: portfolio risk reasoning ✅

✓ Trust and compliance by design

The energy function naturally filters implausible outputs. Predictions are anchored to the learned world model — not free-form generation — making them more auditable and reliable.

Generative LLM: Output: confident but hallucinated claim ❌ JEPA world model: Output: high-energy (implausible) → rejected Output: low-energy (grounded) → surfaced ✅ Audit: traceable to world-model state

✓ Multimodal without model sprawl

Rather than separate models for text, image, and tabular data, JEPA uses a shared representation backbone with modality-specific encoders — one system, all data types.

Today: text model + vision model + tabular model JEPA: one backbone, three encoders shared latent representation space → cross-modal reasoning for free ✅

✓ Continual learning from live CRM data

As new unlabeled interactions arrive daily, the JEPA model can continue self-supervised pretraining — improving automatically without any annotation effort.

Every new case, email, opportunity: → Automatic SSL training signal → Model improves continuously → No labeling team required → Org-specific, proprietary intelligence ✅
