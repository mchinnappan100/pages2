Issues of Public Read/Write for External Users in Salesforce

Giving public read/write access to external users (such as Experience Cloud, Partner, Customer, or Guest users) can create serious security, compliance, and data integrity risks. Below are the main concerns:

  • Data Exposure: External users could see all records of an object, exposing sensitive data.
  • Unauthorized Data Modification: They could update or delete records they don’t own, leading to tampering.
  • Compliance Violations: Could breach GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA, and other regulations.
  • Data Integrity Risks: Accidental duplicates or corrupted records could spread through integrations.
  • Increased Attack Surface: Attackers may exploit weak authentication or inject malicious data.
  • Audit Challenges: Harder to track “who changed what.”
  • Contradicts Best Practices: Salesforce recommends the Principle of Least Privilege.

✅ Best Practices

Instead of public read/write, follow these approaches:

  • Set OWD to Private or Public Read-Only.
  • Use Sharing Rules for controlled access.
  • Apply Apex Managed Sharing for fine-grained control.
  • Assign Permission Sets selectively.
  • Use Sharing Sets to restrict Experience Cloud users to their own records.

⚠️ In short: Public Read/Write for external users = data leakage + compliance risk + security breach waiting to happen.
