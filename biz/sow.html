What is a Statement of Work?

A Statement of Work (SOW) is the foundation of a project contract that outlines all key aspects of work between a client and a service provider. It provides a detailed understanding of what will be delivered, how it will be done, and when it must be completed.

What a SOW Covers

1. Introduction

Background, project objectives, and purpose of the engagement.

2. Scope of Work

Detailed description of what‚Äôs included (and excluded) in the project.

3. Deliverables

Tangible outputs ‚Äî reports, integrations, systems, or assets.

4. Schedule & Milestones

Timeline with key phases and delivery checkpoints.

5. Roles & Responsibilities

Defines who does what ‚Äî client vs. vendor responsibilities.

6. Acceptance Criteria

Defines how deliverables will be tested, reviewed, and approved.

7. Pricing & Payment

Defines payment structure ‚Äî fixed price, T&M, or milestone-based.

8. Change Control

Process for handling any scope or requirement changes.

9. Legal & Compliance

References master service agreements, confidentiality, and IP rights.

Types of SOWs

1. Design / Detail SOW

Specifies exact methods and processes to follow; common in engineering projects.

2. Level of Effort / T&M SOW

Focuses on labor hours and skill levels rather than fixed deliverables.

3. Performance-Based SOW

Defines desired outcomes, giving flexibility in how results are achieved.
