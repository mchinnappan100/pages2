üß© Design / Detail SOW Template

This type of SOW defines *how* the work must be performed. Commonly used in engineering, IT system builds, or manufacturing projects.

Template Outline

  • Purpose & Background: Explain project goals and objectives.
  • Scope of Work: Describe detailed technical tasks, methods, and standards.
  • Deliverables: List tangible outputs with formats and deadlines.
  • Assumptions: Note dependencies such as access or data availability.
  • Acceptance Criteria: Define validation and sign-off process.
  • Timeline: Specify milestones and responsibilities.
  • Pricing & Payment: Typically fixed fee with milestone-based payments.
  • Change Control: Define process for approving scope/design changes.

Project: Salesforce Data Cloud Integration

Deliverables: Design docs, data pipelines, test reports

Timeline: 6 weeks

‚è±Ô∏è Level of Effort (Time & Materials) SOW Template

This SOW type defines resource roles, hours, and rates rather than fixed deliverables. Ideal for consulting, advisory, or ongoing support work.

Template Outline

  • Objective: Define high-level goals (e.g., provide Salesforce Data Cloud advisory).
  • Scope of Work: Outline effort areas (e.g., ETL setup, data modeling, troubleshooting).
  • Resource Plan: Specify roles, estimated hours, and rates.
  • Deliverables: May include progress reports, documentation, or knowledge transfer sessions.
  • Schedule: Define activity durations and sequencing.
  • Invoicing: Monthly billing based on actual hours.
  • Assumptions: Remote work, client provides system access, etc.

üìò Example

Project: Salesforce Data Cloud Advisory Engagement

Resources: Data Architect (20 hrs/wk), Developer (30 hrs/wk)

Duration: 8 weeks

üéØ Performance-Based SOW Template

Focuses on measurable *outcomes* rather than detailed activities. Often used when vendors are paid for achieving results.

Template Outline

  • Purpose: Define the desired business outcomes.
  • Performance Metrics: Define measurable KPIs and success targets.
  • Scope: Describe what areas are covered to meet objectives.
  • Deliverables: Define results expected, not how to achieve them.
  • Responsibilities: Clarify client and vendor obligations.
  • Payment Terms: Tie payments to outcome achievement.
  • Reporting: Weekly or milestone-based progress updates.
  • Acceptance Criteria: Based on verified performance data.
