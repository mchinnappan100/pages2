Top health benefits

High in protein

Chickpeas are a great plant-based protein source (~19g per 100g cooked), supporting muscle repair, energy, and satiety.

Rich in fiber

Provides soluble and insoluble fiber (~8g per 100g cooked) to support digestion, regulate blood sugar, and feed gut microbiota.

Heart health

High fiber and potassium contribute to lower cholesterol and healthy blood pressure levels.

Low glycemic index

Slow-digesting carbs help maintain steady blood sugar levels and provide sustained energy.

Packed with minerals

Chickpeas provide iron, magnesium, and zinc â€” essential for energy production, immunity, and bone health.

Supports weight management

Protein and fiber increase fullness and reduce overall calorie intake.

Easy ways to enjoy chickpeas

Hummus

Blend chickpeas with tahini, lemon, garlic, and olive oil for a creamy dip.

Roasted snacks

Roast chickpeas with spices for a crunchy, protein-packed snack.

Salads & stews

Add chickpeas to salads, curries, and stews for extra protein and fiber.

Tips & cautions

  • Soaking: Soak dried chickpeas overnight to reduce cooking time and improve digestibility.
  • Storage: Store cooked chickpeas in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 5 days.
  • Digestive comfort: Rinse canned chickpeas and cook thoroughly to reduce gas.
