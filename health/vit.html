L-Citrulline

Nonessential Amino Acid

L-citrulline is a nonessential amino acid naturally found in watermelon that plays a crucial role in cardiovascular health and athletic performance. Once ingested, the body converts L-citrulline into L-arginine, which then boosts nitric oxide (NO) production.

Key Benefits

  • Enhances blood flow by relaxing blood vessels
  • Supports heart health and cardiovascular function
  • Improves athletic performance and endurance
  • More effective than L-arginine at increasing NO levels
  • Helps remove ammonia from the body
  • Aids in muscle recovery after exercise
Natural Sources: Watermelon (highest concentration), cucumber, pumpkin, gourds
Common Uses: Exercise performance supplements, blood pressure management, circulation support

Vitamin K2 & D3

The Synergistic Duo

Vitamins K2 and D3 work together synergistically to support bone and cardiovascular health. While vitamin D3 helps your body absorb calcium, vitamin K2 ensures that calcium goes to the right places‚Äîyour bones and teeth‚Äîrather than accumulating in your arteries.

Vitamin D3 Benefits

  • Enhances calcium absorption in the gut
  • Supports immune system function
  • Promotes bone health and density
  • Helps regulate mood and mental health
  • Supports muscle function

Vitamin K2 Benefits

  • Activates proteins that regulate calcium placement
  • Directs calcium to bones and away from arteries
  • Supports cardiovascular health
  • Works with D3 to prevent arterial calcification
  • Promotes dental health
D3 Sources: Sunlight exposure, fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified foods
K2 Sources: Fermented foods (natto), cheese, egg yolks, grass-fed dairy

CoQ10

Coenzyme Q10 - Ubiquinone

Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10) is a naturally occurring antioxidant compound that's essential for energy production in every cell of your body. It's particularly concentrated in organs with high energy demands like the heart, liver, and kidneys.

Key Benefits

  • Crucial for cellular energy (ATP) production
  • Supports heart health and cardiovascular function
  • Powerful antioxidant protecting cells from damage
  • May help reduce effects of aging
  • Supports brain health and cognitive function
  • May improve exercise performance
  • Important for those taking statin medications
Natural Sources: Organ meats (heart, liver), fatty fish, whole grains, spinach, broccoli
Note: CoQ10 levels naturally decline with age, and production can be reduced by certain medications, making supplementation often beneficial for adults over 40.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen.
