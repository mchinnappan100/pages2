Top health benefits

Heart health

Rich in monounsaturated fats and vitamin E, almonds may help lower LDL cholesterol and support cardiovascular health.

Brain & cognitive support

Vitamin E, healthy fats, and magnesium contribute to improved memory, cognition, and neural protection.

Bone health

High in calcium, magnesium, and phosphorus â€” almonds support strong bones and teeth.

Weight management

Protein, fiber, and healthy fats promote satiety, reducing overall calorie intake.

Antioxidant properties

Vitamin E and polyphenols help protect cells from oxidative stress and inflammation.

Blood sugar control

Almonds have a low glycemic index and may help stabilize blood sugar levels when included in meals.

Tips & cautions

  • Storage: Store almonds in an airtight container in the fridge or freezer to prevent rancidity.
  • Portion control: Due to high calorie density, stick to small servings (~23 almonds).
  • Allergies: Avoid almonds if you have a tree nut allergy.
  • Versatility: Use in sweet or savory dishes for added nutrition and crunch.
