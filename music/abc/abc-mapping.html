🎼 Carnatic ↔ Western Mapping Using ABC Notation

Understanding how Carnatic swaras map to Western notes and how to write them using ABC notation.

Introduction

Carnatic music uses the solfege syllables Sa, Ri, Ga, Ma, Pa, Da, Ni to represent musical pitches. Western music uses notes A–G. With a reference pitch (typically Sa = C), we can map Carnatic notes to Western notes and represent them using ABC notation.

Basic Swara Mapping

Carnatic Western ABC Notation
Sa C C
Ri D D
Ga E E
Ma F F
Pa G G
Da A A
Ni B B

Carnatic Scale Example

Carnatic ascending scale (Sa Ri Ga Ma Pa Da Ni Sa): 

S R G M | P D N S

Equivalent ABC notation: 

X:1
T:Carnatic Scale
M:4/4
K:C
C D E F | G A B c |

Raga Mohanam Example

Raga Mohanam (a pentatonic raga used in many Carnatic compositions):

Swara pattern: 

S R G P D S

ABC notation equivalent: 

X:2
T:Raga Mohanam
M:4/4
K:C
C D E G A c |

Carnatic Phrase Example

Short melodic phrase inspired by Carnatic compositions such as those by Tyagaraja.

T:Carnatic Phrase
M:4/4
K:C
C D E G | A G E D |
C2 E G | A4 |

Summary

  • Carnatic swaras can map directly to Western notes.
  • ABC notation provides a simple way to represent music using text.
  • Using libraries like abcjs, we can render and play these notations in browsers.
