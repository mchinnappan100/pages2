Overview

Broccoli (Brassica oleracea var. italica) is a cruciferous vegetable closely related to cabbage, kale, and cauliflower. Originally from the Mediterranean region, broccoli has become one of the most popular vegetables worldwide, valued for its crunchy texture, mild flavor, and exceptional nutritional profile.

Health Benefits

  • Rich in Nutrients: High in vitamins C, K, A, and folate, supporting immunity and bone health.
  • Cancer-Fighting Compounds: Contains sulforaphane, a powerful antioxidant that helps detoxify harmful substances.
  • Supports Heart Health: Promotes healthy cholesterol levels and reduces inflammation in arteries.
  • Boosts Immunity: Vitamin C strengthens the immune system and enhances collagen production.
  • Aids Digestion: High in fiber, supporting gut health and promoting regular bowel movements.

Pollination

Broccoli is primarily insect-pollinated, especially by bees and flies. However, since broccoli is usually harvested before flowering, pollination mainly matters for seed production rather than for the edible part.

  • Flowering: When allowed to bolt, broccoli produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
  • Pollinators: Bees, hoverflies, and butterflies play key roles in transferring pollen between flowers.
