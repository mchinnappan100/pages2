Overview

Health Benefits

  • Rich in Antioxidants: Tomatoes are loaded with lycopene, which helps fight free radicals and reduces the risk of chronic diseases.
  • Heart Health: Lycopene and potassium in tomatoes help lower cholesterol and blood pressure.
  • Skin Health: Vitamin C and beta-carotene protect the skin from sun damage and improve texture.
  • Supports Vision: Tomatoes contain lutein and zeaxanthin, which help prevent macular degeneration.
  • Boosts Immunity: High vitamin C content strengthens the immune system.

Pollination

Tomatoes are primarily self-pollinating, meaning each flower contains both male and female parts. However, gentle vibration or wind helps the pollen move between these parts.

  • Natural Pollination: Wind and bees are the main pollinators for outdoor tomatoes.
  • Hand Pollination: For greenhouse or indoor plants, gently shake flowers or use a small brush to transfer pollen manually.
  • Best Time: Pollination works best during mid-morning when flowers are open and dry.
