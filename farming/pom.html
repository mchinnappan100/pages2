How to identify a Pomegranate

Look for these distinctive features across leaves, flowers, fruit, stems, and habit.

Leaves

Glossy, narrow oval leaves 1–3" long, usually opposite on the stem, smooth texture.

Flowers

Bright orange-red tubular flowers with crinkled petals; late spring–summer bloom.

Fruit

Round fruit with a crown (calyx) on top; thick leathery skin; inside are juicy arils.

Stem & Habit

Reddish young stems, possibly thorny; becomes a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree (6–20 ft).

Quick ID Checklist
  • ✅ Narrow, glossy leaves
  • ✅ Orange-red tubular flowers
  • ✅ Crowned round fruit
  • ✅ Thorny young branches
When to look

Flowers late spring–summer; fruits late summer into fall. Best ID when flowering or fruiting.

USDA Hardiness

Ideal Zones: 8–10

Zone 7 possible with winter protection. For colder zones, grow in containers and overwinter indoors.

Cold tolerance

Mature plants tolerate brief dips to about 10–15°F (-12 to -9°C). Young plants are frost-sensitive.

Care Tips

  • • Full sun (6+ hours) for best fruiting.
  • • Well-drained soil; moderate watering.
  • • Mulch in winter for zone 7 protection.
  • • Prune in spring to shape; avoid winter pruning.

Try these varieties

‘Wonderful’ (common), ‘Parfianka’ (sweet), ‘Russian 26’ (cold-hardy), ‘Nana’ (dwarf).
