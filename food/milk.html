When to choose Full Milk

  • Richer coffee, lattes, and cappuccinos
  • Baking where fat improves crumb and mouthfeel
  • Feeding young children (<2 years consult pediatrician)
  • Cooking creamy sauces and custards

When to choose 1% Milk

  • Lower-calorie diets
  • Pouring on cereal or using in smoothies
  • When you want dairy with less saturated fat
  • General day-to-day drinking

Quick Verdict

Choose Full Milk when you want creaminess and flavor; choose 1% Milk when you want fewer calories while keeping protein and calcium. Both provide similar protein and calcium per cup.
