Top health benefits

High in dietary fiber

Pears are an excellent source of soluble and insoluble fiber. A medium pear provides about 5‚Äì6 grams of fiber, which supports digestion, regularity, and satiety.

Vitamin C & antioxidants

Pears supply vitamin C and plant polyphenols that protect cells from oxidative stress and support the immune system.

Supports gut health

The fiber in pears feeds beneficial gut bacteria, producing short-chain fatty acids that support colon health and metabolism.

Low allergenicity (often)

Many people tolerate pears well compared with other fruits; however, oral allergy syndrome can occur in sensitive individuals.

Hydrating & low-calorie

Pears have high water content and are relatively low in calories, making them a refreshing snack that helps with hydration.

May support weight management

Fiber and water increase fullness, which can reduce overall calorie intake when included in a balanced diet.

  • Ripeness: Press near the stem ‚Äî slight give indicates ripeness. Store unripe pears at room temperature and refrigerate once ripe.
