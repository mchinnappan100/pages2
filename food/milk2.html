Recommended Type

For most toddlers between ages 1 and 2, whole milk (full milk) is recommended. The higher fat content supports brain and nervous system development.

  • Provides essential dietary fat for growth.
  • Helps in absorbing fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K).
  • Offers a richer source of calories for active toddlers.

When to Switch to 1%

After age 2, you can transition to 1% or 2% milk if your pediatrician agrees and your child has a balanced diet. This helps reduce saturated fat intake while maintaining calcium and protein.

  • Ensure other dietary sources provide healthy fats (avocados, nut butters, etc.).
  • Gradually mix full milk and 1% milk to ease the transition.
  • Always check with your pediatrician before switching.
