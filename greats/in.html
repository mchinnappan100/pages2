Isaac Newton

The Architect of Classical Physics

Isaac Newton laid the foundations of modern physics, mathematics, and astronomy. His laws of motion and universal gravitation explained how the universe moves — from falling apples to orbiting planets.

His work defined science for over 300 years and remains essential today.

Quick Facts

  • Born: January 4, 1643
  • Origin: England
  • Fields: Physics, Mathematics, Astronomy
  • Known For: Gravity, Laws of Motion, Calculus
  • Book: Principia Mathematica (1687)

The Three Laws of Motion

First Law: An object remains at rest or in motion unless acted upon by a force.
Second Law: Force = Mass × Acceleration (F = ma)
Third Law: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

Universal Gravitation — The Force That Shapes the Cosmos

Newton discovered that every object attracts every other object with a force called gravity.

F = G (m₁m₂) / r²

This explains falling objects, planetary orbits, and the motion of the universe.

Inventor of Calculus

Newton invented calculus to describe motion and change.

Calculus is essential for physics, engineering, AI, and modern science.

Discoveries in Light and Optics

  • Discovered white light contains all colors
  • Explained rainbows
  • Built the first reflecting telescope
  • Founded modern optics

Principia Mathematica — The Greatest Scientific Book

Published in 1687, Principia unified motion, gravity, and mathematics into one framework.

It became the foundation of classical physics.
