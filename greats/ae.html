Albert Einstein

The Mind That Reshaped Space and Time

Albert Einstein transformed our understanding of the universe. His theories of relativity redefined space, time, gravity, and energy, laying the foundation for modern physics and cosmology.

His equation E = mc² revealed that mass and energy are interchangeable — one of the most profound discoveries in human history.

Quick Facts

  • Born: March 14, 1879
  • Origin: Ulm, Germany
  • Field: Theoretical Physics
  • Famous Equation: E = mc²
  • Award: Nobel Prize in Physics (1921)

Theory of Relativity — Redefining Reality

Einstein developed two revolutionary theories:

Special Relativity (1905)

Time and space are relative. Time slows down at high speeds.

General Relativity (1915)

Gravity is the curvature of spacetime caused by mass.

E = mc² — The Most Famous Equation

E = mc²

This equation shows that mass can be converted into energy. It explains nuclear energy, stars, and the power of the universe.

Photoelectric Effect — Birth of Quantum Mechanics

Einstein explained how light can behave as particles (photons), which earned him the Nobel Prize.

This discovery enabled modern technologies like solar panels and quantum physics.
