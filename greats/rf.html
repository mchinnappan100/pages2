Richard Feynman

The Great Explainer of the Universe

Richard Feynman was one of the most brilliant physicists of the 20th century. He transformed our understanding of quantum mechanics, helped build the atomic bomb, and became the most beloved teacher of physics in history.

His ability to explain complex ideas simply made him legendary.

Quick Facts

  • Born: May 11, 1918
  • Origin: New York, USA
  • Field: Theoretical Physics
  • Famous For: Quantum Electrodynamics (QED)
  • Award: Nobel Prize in Physics (1965)

Quantum Electrodynamics — Understanding Light and Matter

Feynman revolutionized physics by explaining how light and matter interact. His theory, Quantum Electrodynamics (QED), is the most accurate theory in science.

It explains how electrons and photons interact at the quantum level.

Feynman Diagrams — Visualizing the Invisible

Feynman invented diagrams that allow physicists to visualize particle interactions.

The Manhattan Project

Feynman worked at Los Alamos on the Manhattan Project, contributing to the development of the atomic bomb.

His computational techniques helped simulate nuclear reactions.

The Greatest Physics Teacher

His lectures at Caltech became legendary and were published as The Feynman Lectures on Physics.

  • Explained physics with clarity
  • Made complex ideas simple
  • Inspired generations of scientists
  • Created intuitive understanding

Vision of Quantum Computing

Feynman was the first to propose quantum computers.

He realized classical computers could not efficiently simulate quantum physics, but quantum computers could.
