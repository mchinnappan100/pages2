Srinivasa Ramanujan

The Genius Who Saw Infinity

Srinivasa Ramanujan was one of the most extraordinary mathematical minds in history. With almost no formal training, he discovered deep mathematical truths that continue to shape modern mathematics, physics, and computer science.

His intuition was so powerful that many of his discoveries were decades ahead of their time.

Quick Facts

  • Born: December 22, 1887
  • Origin: Erode, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Field: Pure Mathematics
  • Specialties: Number Theory, Infinite Series
  • Legacy: Revolutionary mathematical formulas

Mathematical Discoveries Beyond Imagination

Ramanujan discovered over 3,900 mathematical results, many without proofs. His formulas were later proven correct by the world's greatest mathematicians.

Major Contributions

  • Partition functions
  • Infinite series expansions
  • Modular forms
  • Continued fractions
  • Highly composite numbers

Impact Today

  • String theory and physics
  • Cryptography
  • Black hole entropy theory
  • Computer algorithms
  • Prime number research

Master of Infinite Series

Ramanujan discovered extremely fast converging infinite series to compute π (pi).

1/π = (2√2 / 9801) × Σ ( (4k)! (1103 + 26390k) ) / ( (k!)⁴ 396⁴ᵏ )

This formula is still used in modern supercomputers to calculate trillions of digits of π.

Partnership with G. H. Hardy

British mathematician G. H. Hardy recognized Ramanujan's genius immediately. He brought Ramanujan to Cambridge University, where together they produced groundbreaking mathematical work.

Hardy later said Ramanujan was in a class with Euler and Gauss.

Pure Intuition — A Rare Gift

Ramanujan often saw mathematical truths instantly, without formal derivations.

  • Visualized infinite patterns mentally
  • Derived formulas without formal training
  • Produced results decades ahead of mathematics
  • Worked entirely from intuition and insight
