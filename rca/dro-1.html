Section 01

DRO Fundamentals

01
🎯

What is DRO?

Dynamic Revenue Orchestrator is Salesforce's intelligent order fulfillment engine, part of Agentforce Revenue Management (formerly Revenue Cloud).

Coordination layer between sales and back-office fulfillment
Manages end-to-end order-to-cash lifecycle
Decomposes complex orders into fulfillment components
Automates cross-system task orchestration

A customer purchases a cloud software bundle. Without DRO, billing, provisioning, and support teams manually coordinate via email. With DRO, a single order triggers an automated plan that provisions licenses, activates support, and generates the invoice — all tracked in one place.

Result: faster fulfillment, zero manual handoffs, full audit trail.
⚠️

Why Organizations Need DRO

Modern businesses sell complex bundles across channels. Without orchestration, fulfillment breaks down at system and team boundaries.

Fragmented systems require manual coordination
Complex product bundles have interdependent timelines
Revenue recognition depends on fulfillment completion
Errors and delays damage customer experience

A customer buys a laptop + antivirus + extended warranty + installation service. Each item involves a different team and system. Without DRO: spreadsheets, missed steps, and angry customers. With DRO: a single orchestration plan manages all dependencies automatically.

Every component tracked, every dependency enforced, no step skipped.
🌐

Multi-System Landscape

A single order often touches many systems. DRO acts as the orchestration hub, eliminating point-to-point integration spaghetti.

CRM, ERP, billing, and inventory systems
Logistics providers and third-party vendors
External APIs and fulfillment platforms
Reduced custom point-to-point integrations
Order → DRO Orchestration Hub ├── SAP ERP (inventory allocation) ├── Zuora (billing & invoicing) ├── ServiceNow (support ticket) └── FedEx API (shipment tracking)
One orchestration plan replaces four separate integrations.
📈

Revenue Lifecycle Alignment

Revenue cannot be recognized until fulfillment obligations are met. DRO bridges the gap between selling and billing.

Fulfillment completion triggers revenue recognition
Real-time visibility into order status
Faster billing through automated completion signals
Compliance with ASC 606 / IFRS 15 obligations

A SaaS company can only recognize subscription revenue after successful license provisioning. DRO automatically notifies the billing system the moment provisioning completes, triggering the invoice without any manual intervention.

Days-to-invoice drops from 5 days to same-day.
Section 02

The Two Core Pillars

02
🔀

Order Decomposition

The process of breaking a commercial order into discrete technical fulfillment components, each independently tracked and managed.

Maps commercial line items to fulfillment tasks
Enables parallel fulfillment activities
Each component retains linkage to parent order
Supports specialized downstream fulfillment systems
Order: "Enterprise Cloud Bundle" ↓ Decomposed into: ├── [HW-001] Laptop shipment ├── [SW-001] Antivirus license activation ├── [SW-002] Office suite provisioning ├── [SVC-001] Warranty registration └── [SVC-002] Support entitlement setup
Each item fulfilled independently, tracked against the parent order.
🎼

Order Orchestration

The sequencing and automation of fulfillment activities across systems, enforcing dependencies and SLAs from order submission to completion.

Defines tasks, dependencies, and execution order
Enforces prerequisite completion before next step
Built-in SLA targets and breach detection
Exception handling and fallout routing
1Verify payment cleared
2Allocate inventory in ERP
3Ship hardware (parallel)
4Activate software licenses (parallel)
5Register warranty & support
6Generate invoice & notify customer
Steps 3 and 4 run in parallel; step 5 only starts when both complete.
📋

Fulfillment Plans

Reusable templates that define how specific products or services are delivered — activities, dependencies, and success criteria packaged for reuse.

Encapsulate delivery logic for product families
Applied automatically at order submission
Versioned and maintainable by fulfillment designers
Reduces configuration effort across product catalog

A "Cloud CRM Subscription" fulfillment plan is built once, covering tenant provisioning, user license assignment, and onboarding email. Every time a rep closes a deal for that product, the same plan executes automatically — no manual setup required.

Build once, apply everywhere across all orders for that product.
🚨

Fallout Management

Proactive failure detection and remediation that keeps orders moving even when individual fulfillment steps encounter errors.

Automatic failure detection across all tasks
Configurable exception and escalation workflows
Routes corrective actions to the right operator
SLA compliance maintained through breach alerts

A SIM provisioning API call fails mid-fulfillment. DRO detects the failure, pauses dependent steps to avoid cascading errors, opens a fallout case for the provisioning team, and starts the SLA breach countdown. The team resolves it and resumes the plan — the customer's order continues without starting over.

Failures are contained, not catastrophic. The order recovers, not restarts.
Section 03

Components & Runtime

03
⏱️

SLA Monitoring

Define service-level targets for every fulfillment task and receive proactive alerts before and after deadlines are breached.

Per-task and per-order SLA targets
Warning thresholds before breach
Breach dashboards for operational teams
SLA data feeds into revenue recognition reporting
SLA Config — Device Shipment Task: Warning at: 4 hours remaining Breach at: 8 hours from order submit On breach: Escalate to Ops Manager Open Priority-1 fallout case
Operators see a warning at hour 4, giving time to intervene before the breach.
🔌

Integration Architecture

DRO connects to external systems via standard Salesforce integration patterns, acting as the orchestration hub without owning fulfillment logic.

Outbound REST/SOAP callouts to ERP and billing
Platform Events for async fulfillment signals
Named Credentials for secure endpoint management
Flow and Apex extensions for custom logic

DRO sends an inventory allocation request to SAP via a REST callout. SAP responds asynchronously via a Platform Event when allocation is confirmed. DRO listens for that event and advances the orchestration plan — no polling loop, no synchronous blocking.

Async integration keeps DRO non-blocking and scalable at high order volumes.
📊

Operational Visibility

Real-time dashboards and work queues give every stakeholder a live view of order progress, exceptions, and fulfillment metrics.

Fulfillment queue views for operators
Order timeline and task status tracking
Fallout case management dashboards
Cycle time and SLA compliance reporting

A fulfillment manager opens the DRO dashboard on Monday morning and sees 3 orders with SLA warnings, 1 fallout case awaiting assignment, and the average fulfillment cycle time for the past week — all from a single screen without querying multiple systems.

Single pane of glass replaces spreadsheets, emails, and cross-system queries.
🤖

Agentforce Integration

DRO integrates with Agentforce to enable AI-assisted fallout resolution, predictive SLA management, and intelligent order routing.

AI-suggested remediation steps for fallout cases
Predictive SLA breach detection using order history
Automated operator assignment based on workload
Natural language order status for customer-facing agents

An Agentforce agent detects that a provisioning task is trending toward a breach 2 hours ahead of the SLA deadline. It automatically reassigns the task to an available operator, sends a proactive customer notification, and logs the intervention — all without human oversight.

AI shifts DRO from reactive fallout handling to proactive exception prevention.
Section 04

Key Roles & Responsibilities

04
⚙️

DRO Administrator

Owns the installation, configuration, and ongoing health of the DRO platform. The operational backbone of any DRO deployment.

Installs and upgrades managed packages
Configures fallout rules and SLA thresholds
Manages user permissions and queue assignments
Monitors system health and troubleshoots issues

After a quarterly release, the DRO Administrator reviews the upgrade guide, runs a sandbox deployment, validates SLA configurations, updates fallout routing rules for a new product line, and certifies the production deployment.

Admins own the platform; designers own the logic; operators own the runtime.
✏️

Fulfillment Designer

Designs the decomposition patterns and orchestration plans that power order fulfillment — the architect of the revenue engine.

Designs product decomposition mappings
Builds and tests fulfillment plans and task flows
Defines dependency rules and parallel task groups
Partners with integration teams for external callouts

When the product team launches a new IoT device bundle, the Fulfillment Designer maps each bundle component to fulfillment tasks, defines which tasks can run in parallel versus sequentially, builds the plan in DRO, and validates it against 10 test orders before production launch.

Designers translate product requirements into executable orchestration logic.
👷

Fulfillment Operator

Monitors and executes fulfillment tasks at runtime, resolving manual steps and managing exception cases as orders flow through the system.

Works fulfillment queues for manual task completion
Investigates and resolves fallout cases
Escalates unresolvable exceptions to designers
Provides feedback on recurring failure patterns

An operator starts their shift and sees 12 items in the fulfillment queue: 8 routine manual approvals, 3 fallout cases requiring investigation, and 1 SLA warning. They clear the approvals first, investigate the fallouts, and raise a pattern they noticed to the designer — three orders failed at the same step.

Operators are the human-in-the-loop layer that keeps exceptions from becoming backlogs.
🏗️

Salesforce Architect

Designs the end-to-end DRO solution architecture — integration patterns, data model extensions, scalability, and governance framework.

Defines integration topology and API contracts
Designs decomposition strategy across product catalog
Governs fulfillment plan reuse and versioning
Ensures scalability at peak order volumes

Before a DRO implementation begins, the architect runs a discovery sprint with ERP, billing, and logistics stakeholders. They document every integration touchpoint, define async vs. sync patterns per system, create a fulfillment plan taxonomy, and produce an architecture decision record (ADR) before any configuration starts.

Architecture decisions made late cost 10× more to fix than decisions made at the start.
Best Practices

Implementation Best Practices

BP

✓ Design for Reusability

Build fulfillment plans as reusable templates aligned to product families, not individual SKUs. A plan for "Cloud Subscription" covers hundreds of products without duplication.

Good: FulfillmentPlan → "Cloud-SaaS-v2" Covers: 47 subscription products Bad: FulfillmentPlan → "CRM-Pro-SKU-1234" Covers: 1 product

✓ Keep Decomposition Granular

Break orders into the smallest logical fulfillment unit that maps to a real operational responsibility. Too coarse means lost visibility; too fine means operational noise.

✓ Build Fallout Handling First

Assume failures will occur in every integration. Design remediation paths, escalation rules, and retry logic before go-live — not after the first production incident.

✓ Define SLAs for Every Task

Every fulfillment step should have a measurable SLA, even if generous. Undefined SLAs mean no breach detection, no alerts, and no accountability.

✅ SLA defined: Ship hardware within 24h Warn at 18h, breach at 24h ❌ No SLA: Ship hardware "as soon as possible"

✓ Use Async Integration Patterns

Use Platform Events and callbacks for external system communication. Synchronous callouts block the orchestration engine and reduce throughput at scale.

✓ Engage Integration Partners Early

Bring ERP, billing, and logistics teams into design sessions from day one. Late integration discoveries are the top cause of DRO project delays.

✓ Version Your Fulfillment Plans

Treat fulfillment plans like software: version them, test changes in sandbox, and maintain backward compatibility for orders already in flight when plans are updated.

✓ Monitor Operational Metrics

Track fulfillment cycle times, fallout rates, SLA breach percentages, and task completion rates weekly. Metrics reveal systemic issues before they become customer complaints.

Key metrics to track: • Avg fulfillment cycle time • Fallout rate by product/step • SLA breach % by task type • Time-to-invoice from order submit

✓ Test With Realistic Order Volumes

Load-test orchestration plans with production-scale order volumes before go-live. Bottlenecks in external APIs and Apex callouts rarely surface in single-order testing.

✓ Document Decomposition Decisions

Record why each product was decomposed the way it was. Future designers and architects need the reasoning, not just the configuration, when product bundles evolve.
