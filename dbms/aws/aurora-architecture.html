Amazon Aurora Architecture

Overview

Amazon Aurora is a high-performance, fully managed, cloud-native relational database service from AWS. It is MySQL- and PostgreSQL-compatible while delivering up to 5× the throughput of standard MySQL and 3× that of PostgreSQL.

Aurora's core innovation is the decoupling of compute and storage, enabling independent scaling, faster crash recovery, high availability, and massive read scaling with shared storage across replicas.

Aurora stores data in a virtual cluster volume composed of many 10 GB segments. Each segment is replicated six times across three Availability Zones (two copies per AZ). Writes require acknowledgment from 4 out of 6 copies; reads need 3 out of 6. This provides strong durability and fault tolerance (can survive AZ+1 failures).

Aurora Global Database enables low-latency global reads and cross-region disaster recovery. One primary region handles writes; up to 10 secondary regions provide local read access with sub-second replication lag in most cases.

Key Features & Benefits

Compute & Storage Separation

Compute scales independently of storage. Fast crash recovery (often <30s) and no I/O freezes during repairs.

High Availability & Durability

Six copies across three AZs → survives AZ failure + one additional fault. Automatic self-healing storage.

Read Scaling

Up to 15 Aurora Replicas (same region) or 16 per secondary region in Global Database → massive read throughput.

Continuous Backup & Recovery

Automatic incremental backups to S3. Point-in-time recovery down to seconds without performance impact.

Aurora I/O-Optimized

Configuration for I/O-intensive workloads with better price/performance and predictable costs.

Aurora Serverless v2 & Limitless

Automatic scaling from zero to hundreds of thousands of transactions. Limitless offers virtually unlimited scale-out.
