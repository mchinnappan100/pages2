How to Use the IDE

1

Open the Manual Steps IDE

Navigate to the IDE interface and familiarize yourself with the layout. You'll see sections for entering manual steps, viewing generated prompts, and managing your workflow documentation.

Tip: Bookmark the IDE URL for quick access. It works entirely in your browser with no installation required.

2

Enter Your Manual Steps

Document each step of your Salesforce manual process in clear, detailed language. Include:

  • ▸ Action: What needs to be done (e.g., "Create a new Account record")
  • ▸ Details: Specific fields, values, or conditions required
  • ▸ Context: Prerequisites or related information

Example Manual Step:

1. Navigate to Accounts tab
2. Click "New" button
3. Fill in Account Name: "Acme Corporation"
4. Set Account Type: "Customer - Direct"
5. Enter Annual Revenue: "$1,000,000"
6. Click "Save"

Tips & Best Practices

Writing Effective Manual Steps

  • • Be specific about field names, object types, and values
  • • Include any validation rules or business logic requirements
  • • Note any dependencies or prerequisites for each step
  • • Mention error handling or edge cases to consider
  • • Use consistent terminology matching your Salesforce org

Optimizing AI Prompts

  • • Review generated prompts and add org-specific context
  • • Specify the desired output format (Apex, SOQL, clicks, etc.)
  • • Include examples of expected results when helpful
  • • Test prompts with small datasets before scaling up
  • • Iterate on prompts based on AI output quality

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need to install anything?

No! The Manual Steps IDE runs entirely in your browser. Just open the link and start working.

Q: Which AI tools can I use the generated prompts with?

The generated prompts work with any LLM including Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, and other AI platforms that accept text prompts.

Q: Is my data stored or sent anywhere?

No. The IDE works 100% locally in your browser. Your manual steps and generated prompts are never sent to any server and remain private on your device.

Q: Can I save my work?

Yes! You can export your steps and prompts or simply copy them. For persistent storage, save the exported file or use browser bookmarks with pre-filled data (via URL parameters in future updates).

Q: Is it free to use?

Completely free and open source. No sign-up, no limits, no tracking.
