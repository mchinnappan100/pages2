Salesforce Steps Manager

Manual & Feature Guide

Introduction

The **Salesforce Steps Manager** is a native Salesforce app that helps you break down, organize, and track structured multi-step processes (or “steps”) on records. It gives you visibility into where a record is in a process, what’s next, and what has been completed.

Step Definitions & Templates

Create step templates to define the stages of a process: what steps are required, conditions for each step, and the order (sequential or parallel).

Interactive vs Background Steps

Support for user-interactive steps (requiring user input) and background steps (automated, no user action needed), configurable per process.

Queues, Groups & Assignments

Assign steps to users, queues, or groups. This ensures the right people are responsible for the right steps in each process.

Progress Tracking & Status

Track which steps are open, in progress, or completed across records. Visualize the progress on step records or using dashboards.

Conditional Logic & Branching

Use logic to decide which steps apply based on record data, or branch into different step-flows depending on criteria.

Automation & Flow Integration

Integrate with Salesforce Flow to automate step transitions, trigger actions when steps complete, or execute background steps with zero user input.

How It Works

  1. Define a Process: Create a step template that outlines all the steps for a particular business process.
  2. Assign Steps: Choose who owns each step — individual user, queue, or group.
  3. Track Execution: As records move through the process, steps are created, updated, and completed. Users or automation can move steps forward.
  4. Visualize Progress: Use record-level views or dashboards to monitor where things are in the process, see stuck steps, and identify bottlenecks.
  5. Automate Actions: Leverage Flow, Process Builder, or Apex (if needed) to run actions when steps are completed, failed, or timed out.

Key Benefits

  • Clarity & Transparency: Everyone sees exactly what steps are left, who is responsible, and how far along things are.
  • Consistency: Enforce a standardized process for tasks, approvals, onboarding, or any multi-step workflow.
  • Scalability: Easily modify or branch your step templates as your processes evolve.
  • Automation First: Combine with Salesforce Flow to minimize manual work and reduce errors.
  • Reporting & Analytics: Generate reports on step throughput, cycle times, step aging, and more.

Getting Started

To set up Steps Manager in your org:

  • Create a **Step Template**: Navigate to the “Templates” tab and define your process flow.
  • Set up **Assignments**: Decide which users or queues will be responsible for each step.
  • Configure **Automation**: Use Flow to automate step creation, completion, or branching.
  • Enable **Status Tracking**: Add step status fields to page layouts and build reports/dashboards.
  • Train your team: Provide guidance on how to complete or advance steps in their Salesforce UI.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use **branching logic** thoughtfully to avoid overly complex step templates.
  • Limit the number of concurrent steps to make tracking simpler.
  • Use **Flow Validation** to ensure that business rules are met before a step can complete.
  • Regularly monitor **step durations** to identify slow or stuck steps.
  • Leverage **dashboard charts** to visualize step progression and step drop-offs.
