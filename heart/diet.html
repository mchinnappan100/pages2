7 Heart-Healthy Foods for Stent Patients

Nutrition tips to support heart recovery and reduce future risks

Foods that support heart health ❤️

After a heart stent procedure, it's important to eat nutrient-rich foods that promote cardiovascular health, reduce inflammation, and improve blood circulation. Here are 7 top choices:

1. Fatty Fish

Salmon, mackerel, and sardines are rich in omega-3 fatty acids that reduce inflammation and lower the risk of blood clots.

2. Leafy Greens

Spinach, kale, and Swiss chard provide vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that support vascular health.

3. Berries

Blueberries, strawberries, and raspberries contain anthocyanins and fiber that help lower blood pressure and cholesterol.

4. Oats and Whole Grains

Oatmeal, quinoa, and brown rice provide soluble fiber that reduces LDL cholesterol.

5. Nuts and Seeds

Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds are sources of healthy fats, fiber, and plant sterols that benefit heart health.

6. Legumes

Chickpeas, lentils, and beans provide protein, fiber, and potassium to improve circulation and reduce blood pressure.

7. Olive Oil

Extra virgin olive oil is rich in monounsaturated fats and antioxidants that protect blood vessels and reduce inflammation.

Tips for a heart-healthy diet

  • Reduce salt, processed foods, and added sugars.
  • Eat a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables daily.
  • Include lean protein sources and healthy fats.
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet alongside regular check-ups.
