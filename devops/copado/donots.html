Critical DO NOTs
for Branch Integrity

Protecting your pipeline from Dev → QA → UAT → Main. These rules exist because every shortcut taken today becomes tomorrow's merge conflict.

4 Environments

Golden Rule

The branch is the source of truth. If metadata changes directly inside an org, the pipeline becomes unreliable and drift propagates downstream. Every rule below protects this principle.

01 — 07 The Critical Rules
01
🔒
Do NOT Modify Metadata Directly in QA / UAT / Production
HIGH RISK

Developers should never update flows, validation rules, custom metadata, permissions, layouts, Apex, or config directly in shared environments.

Why it matters Direct org changes create branch drift. Promotions later overwrite, miss, or conflict with the actual org state — making your pipeline unreliable at the worst possible moment.
02
🚪
Do NOT Grant Broad Deployment Access
HIGH RISK

Developers should not have unrestricted edit or deploy access to QA, UAT, or Mainline orgs.

Why it matters Broad access bypasses Copado promotion controls and eliminates deployment traceability. You lose the audit trail that compliance and governance depend on.
03
🐛
Do NOT Fix Bugs Directly in Higher Environments
HIGH RISK

Hotfixes made directly in QA or UAT — "just to unblock testing" — almost always create future merge conflicts and introduce changes that get silently lost.

Why it matters A direct fix in QA that isn't committed to a feature branch will be overwritten the next time a promotion happens. The bug reappears and no one knows why.
04
🚦
Do NOT Skip Promotion Gates
HIGH RISK

Never manually mark user stories complete or bypass the Ready-to-Promote validation checks, even under deadline pressure.

Why it matters Quality Gates exist to catch problems before they reach Production. Bypassing them transfers the risk from the pipeline to the org — at the worst possible time.
05
📦
Do NOT Bundle Unvalidated Stories
MEDIUM RISK

Bundling unrelated user stories into a single promotion masks hidden dependencies and causes metadata collisions downstream.

Why it matters When the combined deployment fails, isolating which story caused the failure is expensive and time-consuming. Small, focused promotions are debuggable promotions.
06
🌊
Do NOT Allow Environment Drift to Persist
HIGH RISK

Any detected drift between a branch and its corresponding org must trigger immediate reconciliation — not a "we'll get to it later" ticket.

Why it matters Drift compounds. A small difference today becomes an irreconcilable conflict by next sprint. The fix is always: retrieve → commit → promote cleanly.
07
📋
Do NOT Ignore Manual Steps Documentation
MEDIUM RISK

Missing documentation for flow activation steps, feature toggles, or post-deployment actions causes production instability despite technically successful deployments.

Why it matters Metadata deploys succeed but the feature is broken because a manual step was forgotten. Every deployment task should include a documented, reviewed checklist.
REFERENCE What Good Looks Like
✅

The Healthy Pipeline Checklist

✓

All changes originate from feature branches — no direct org edits at any stage

✓

Every promotion is traceable to a Copado user story with a full audit trail

✓

Environment access is role-restricted — developers cannot deploy to QA/UAT/Prod directly

✓

Drift detection runs regularly — and detected drift is resolved within the same sprint

✓

Post-deployment manual steps are mandatory — documented, reviewed, and signed off

✓

QA and UAT remain immutable validation mirrors — no ad-hoc changes, ever
